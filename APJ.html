A. P. J. Abdul Kalam


Great Indian scientist and politician who played a leading role in the development of India’s missile and nuclear weapons programs.
A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, in full Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.

Achievements

He served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007

On 18 July 2002, the results were declared. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam became the 11th President by beating his nearest rival Lakshmi Sahgal.

India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV)

In 1969, Kalam was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) where he was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit in July 1980; Kalam had first started work on an expandable rocket project .

Making India a nuclear power.

He thus came to be known as the Missile Man of India for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology. He also played a pivotal organisational, technical, and political role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, the first since the original nuclear test by India in 1974.

Creating a cost-effective stent.

Kalam and Raju used the state of the art defence technology to design what was later known as “Kalam-Raju stent”. The specially designed coil and metal based stent was priced at Rs 7,000, way cheaper than the commonly used stent which is available for Rs 30,000.
